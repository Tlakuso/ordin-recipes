Chakalaka

Description

The origins of the dish is a bit murky but it is thought that it originated in the townships of Johannesburg around the gold mines where mineworkers would create a stew consisting of tinned produce and any fresh vegetables they could obtain with chili. This was eaten with pap. Today it is a very popular dish to accompany a braai (barbeque). It can be eaten cold as a salad or it can be heated. Spicy chakalaka is best served the day after making it to allow the ingredients to mingle and get to know each other.

ingredients

  • 1 large onion finely diced
  • 2 red chilies finely diced
  • 1 green pepper finely diced
  • 4 carrots grated
  • 1/4 cabbage finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes chopped
  • 15ml tomato paste
  • thumb size piece of ginger finely grated
  • 3 cloves of garlic finely chopped
  • 60ml olive oil
  • 5ml durban masala
  • 5–10ml salt (adjust as you are cooking)
  • 10ml white vinegar

Steps

  1. Heat the oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions, peppers and chili and fry for 10 minutes or until turning soft.
  3. Add the cabbage, carrot, tomato, tomato paste, ginger and garlic to the pot and stir to combine.
  4. Add the masala and stir to combine.
  5. Add half the salt and cover the pot. Let simmer on low for 10 to 15 minutes.
  6. Add additional salt to taste and the vinegar.
  7. Cover again and simmer for another 10 minutes
  8. Vegetables should still have a slight crunch but cooked through.
  9. Once done remove the spicy chakalaka to a container and refrigerate overnight for the best flavour. You can also eat it immediately if you can’t wait.
